Dr. Muhammad Yunus (born June 28, 1940) is a Bangladeshi economist, entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate (2006) celebrated for founding Grameen Bank. He pioneered the concept of microcredit, providing small, collateral-free loans to the rural poor, particularly women, to encourage entrepreneurship and break the cycle of poverty. His innovative "social business" model addresses societal needs without focusing solely on profit. In August 2024, he assumed office as the Chief Adviser of Bangladesh's interim government.
